William Shakespeare

William Shakespeare was an English playwright, poet, and actor, widely considered the greatest writer in the English language and the world's pre-eminent dramatist. Born in Stratford-upon-Avon in 1564, he died in the same town in 1616. His prolific output includes 39 plays, 154 sonnets, three long narrative poems, and a few other verses, some of uncertain authorship

Elliott Smith

Steven Paul Smith (August 6, 1969 – October 21, 2003), known as Elliott Smith, was an American musician and singer-songwriter. He was born in Omaha, Nebraska, raised primarily in Texas, and lived much of his life in Portland, Oregon, where he gained popularity. Smith's primary instrument was the guitar, though he also played piano, clarinet, bass guitar, drums, and harmonica

Fyodor Dostoevsky

Fyodor Dostoyevsky (born November 11 [October 30, Old Style], 1821, Moscow, Russia—died February 9 [January 28, Old Style], 1881, St. Petersburg) was a Russian novelist and short-story writer whose psychological penetration into the darkest recesses of the human heart, together with his unsurpassed moments of illumination, had an immense influence on 20th-century fiction.

Kendrick Lamar

Kendrick Lamar Duckworth, known professionally as Kendrick Lamar, is an American rapper, songwriter, and record producer. Born in Compton, California, he gained recognition in the early 2000s under the name K. Dot, eventually signing with Top Dawg Entertainment (TDE). Lamar is widely regarded as one of the greatest rappers of all time, and his work has garnered numerous accolades, including a Pulitzer Prize for Music, 22 Grammy Awards, and a Primetime Emmy Award
